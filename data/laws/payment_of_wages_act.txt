Section 3 - Responsibility for payment of wages

(1) Every employer shall be responsible for the payment of all wages required to be paid under this Act to persons employed by him.

This responsibility applies to different types of employment situations:

(a) In factories, the manager of the factory is responsible for wage payments.

(b) In industrial or other establishments, the person responsible for supervision and control of the establishment is responsible.

(c) In railways (other than factories), the railway administration or a nominated authority is responsible.

(d) In the case of a contractor, a person designated by the contractor who is directly in charge is responsible.

(e) In any other case, a person designated by the employer is responsible for ensuring compliance with the Act.

In all the above cases, the designated or responsible person must ensure proper and timely payment of wages.

(2) If the contractor or designated person fails to make payment of wages, the employer shall remain ultimately responsible for ensuring that wages are paid.

Explanation:
This section ensures that employees always have a legally accountable authority for receiving wages. Even if intermediaries such as contractors are involved, the employer cannot escape responsibility.
