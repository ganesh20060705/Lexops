CASE: Ghaziabad Development Authority v. Balbir Singh
YEAR: 2004
COURT: Supreme Court of India
CITATION: (2004) 5 SCC 65
CATEGORY: Consumer / Real Estate / Deficiency of Service
RULING: The Supreme Court upheld the award of compensation to a flat buyer for delay in possession by the Ghaziabad Development Authority. Held that allottees are consumers under the Consumer Protection Act and delay in handing over possession constitutes deficiency of service. Interest at 18% per annum on deposited amounts was awarded.
KEY PRINCIPLE: Delay in possession entitles flat buyers to compensation and interest under the Consumer Protection Act. Builder/Authority cannot retain money without delivering the property.

CASE: Lucknow Development Authority v. M.K. Gupta
YEAR: 1993
COURT: Supreme Court of India
CITATION: AIR 1994 SC 787
CATEGORY: Consumer / Housing / Deficiency of Service
RULING: Landmark judgment holding that public authorities providing housing are subject to the Consumer Protection Act. Deficiency of service by government bodies is actionable. The Court also held that Consumer Forums can award compensation for mental agony and harassment.
KEY PRINCIPLE: Government housing authorities are liable as service providers under the Consumer Protection Act. Mental agony is compensable.

CASE: Spring Meadows Hospital v. Harjol Ahluwalia
YEAR: 1998
COURT: Supreme Court of India
CITATION: (1998) 4 SCC 39
CATEGORY: Consumer / Medical Negligence
RULING: The Supreme Court held that medical negligence resulting in death of a child entitles the parents to compensation under the Consumer Protection Act. A nurse administering wrong medication and a doctor failing to supervise constituted gross negligence. Parents, as dependants, are consumers even though they themselves did not avail the service.
KEY PRINCIPLE: Medical negligence is actionable under the Consumer Protection Act. Dependants of the patient are also consumers.

CASE: National Insurance Co. Ltd v. Hindustan Safety Glass Works Ltd
YEAR: 2017
COURT: Supreme Court of India
CITATION: (2017) 5 SCC 781
CATEGORY: Consumer / Insurance / Deficiency of Service
RULING: The Supreme Court held that an insurance company repudiating a valid claim without sufficient reason constitutes deficiency of service under the Consumer Protection Act. The insurer is bound by the terms of the policy and cannot impose conditions not mentioned therein to reject a claim.
KEY PRINCIPLE: Unjustified repudiation of insurance claims is deficiency of service. Insurer must settle claims in accordance with policy terms.

CASE: Emaar MGF Land Ltd v. Aftab Singh
YEAR: 2018
COURT: National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission
CITATION: RP No. 4086 of 2017
CATEGORY: Consumer / Real Estate / RERA
RULING: The NCDRC held that a consumer can file a complaint before the Consumer Forum even after RERA came into force, as the remedies are concurrent and not exclusive. Builder liable for delay in possession and must refund with 12% interest or offer possession with compensation.
KEY PRINCIPLE: Consumer Forum and RERA jurisdiction is concurrent; buyer can choose either forum. Interest at 12% for delayed possession.
